Roblox Creator Documentation: 2026 Publishing Standards

Section 1: The "Public" Strategy (Recommended)

To allow random players to find your game via search or direct links, your experience must be set to Public. As of late 2025, accounts must meet at least one "Trust Requirement" to toggle this setting.

Trust Requirements

  • ID Verification: Uploading a government-issued ID via account settings.
  • Verified Purchaser: Having a record of a real-currency purchase (Robux or Gift Cards) since January 1, 2025.
  • Established Status: A history of DevEx or high-engagement milestones.

Steps to Publish

  1. Open Roblox Studio and select File > Publish to Roblox.
  2. Navigate to the Creator Dashboard.
  3. Select your experience and go to Settings > Basic Info.
  4. Complete the Content Maturity Questionnaire (Required for visibility).
  5. Under Permissions, switch the toggle to Public and click Save.

Section 2: The "No-Verify" Workaround (Private Playtesting)

If you prefer not to verify your ID or spend money, you can still host players by manually authorizing them as "Connections." Note: This does not make the game visible to random users; only those you invite can join.

Authorized Access Setup

  • Friendship: You must first be friends with the user on the Roblox platform.
  • Studio Permissions: Open your game in Roblox Studio and click the Collaborate button in the top right.
  • User Search: Search for the specific username of your friend.
  • Set Access: Change their permission level to Play. (Avoid "Edit" unless you want them to change your code).
  • Save: Click Save to apply changes.

Result: When your authorized friend clicks your game link, they will see a green "Play" button instead of a "Permission Denied" error.
