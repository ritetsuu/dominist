        <section>
            <h2>Section 1: The "Public" Strategy (Recommended)</h2>
            <p>To allow random players to find your game via search or direct links, your experience must be set to <strong>Public</strong>. As of early 2026, accounts must meet at least one "Trust Requirement" to toggle this setting.</p>
            
            <h3>Trust Requirements</h3>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>ID Verification:</strong> Uploading a government-issued ID via account settings.</li>
                <li><strong>Verified Purchaser:</strong> Having a record of a real-currency purchase (Robux or Gift Cards) since January 1, 2025.</li>
                <li><strong>Established Status:</strong> A history of DevEx or high-engagement milestones.</li>
            </ul>

            <h3>Steps to Publish</h3>
            <ol>
                <li>Open <strong>Roblox Studio</strong> and select <em>File > Publish to Roblox</em>.</li>
                <li>Navigate to the <a href="https://create.roblox.com/dashboard/creations">Creator Dashboard</a>.</li>
                <li>Select your experience and go to <strong>Settings > Basic Info</strong>.</li>
                <li>Complete the <strong>Content Maturity Questionnaire</strong> (Required for visibility).</li>
                <li>Under <strong>Permissions</strong>, switch the toggle to <strong>Public</strong> and click Save.</li>
            </ol>
        </section>

        <section>
            <h2>Section 2: The "No-Verify" Workaround (Private Playtesting)</h2>
            <p>If you prefer not to verify your ID or spend money, you can still host players by manually authorizing them as "Connections." Note: This does not make the game visible to random users; only those you invite can join.</p>

            <h3>Authorized Access Setup</h3>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>Friendship:</strong> You must first be friends with the user on the Roblox platform.</li>
                <li><strong>Studio Permissions:</strong> Open your game in Roblox Studio and click the <strong>Collaborate</strong> button in the top right.</li>
                <li><strong>User Search:</strong> Search for the specific username of your friend.</li>
                <li><strong>Set Access:</strong> Change their permission level to <strong>Play</strong>. (Avoid "Edit" unless you want them to change your code).</li>
                <li><strong>Save:</strong> Click Save to apply changes.</li>
            </ul>
            <p>When your authorized friend clicks your game link, they will see a green "Play" button instead of a "Permission Denied" error.</p>
        </section>
